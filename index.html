Beef Goulash

RECIPE INSPIRATION: Taming Twins

Nothing says cozy like a big bowl of beef goulash! This comfort food full of rich flavors originated from Hungary, and is all about tender chunks of beef, sweet onions and smoky paprika, all in a savory sauce that tastes like it has been simmering for hours. Serve it over noodles, rice or with a chunk of soft Agege bread, and you'll be hooked forever.

Beef Goulash - absolutely sumptuous!

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 2 hours 30 minutes (oven) or 4 hours (slow cooker)

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 700g diced beef
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons paprika
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 Knorr beef stock cubes
  • 6 medium sized tomatoes, diced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 large red bell pepper, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. For Slow Cooker Method: Put all ingredients except the red peppers into the slow cooker. No browning necessary! Cook for 4-5 hours on HIGH or 6-8 hours on LOW.
  2. Add the peppers: Add the sliced red peppers to the slow cooker and cook for a further 45-60 minutes on HIGH.
  3. For Oven Method: Put all ingredients (including peppers) into a lidded ovenproof pan and cook at 160°C fan/180°C/Gas Mark 4/320°F for 1.5-2.5 hours, depending on the size of your beef cubes.
  4. Check during cooking: After one hour of oven cooking, stir and check. Add a splash of water if the sauce is looking dry.
  5. Serve: Season to taste with additional salt and pepper if needed. Serve hot over mashed potatoes, rice, cooked orzo, or with crusty bread. Top with sour cream and fresh chopped parsley for extra flavor!

Tips & Notes

  • You can make this in an oven if you do not have a slow cooker. The ideal oven settings will be included in the recipe.
  • Caraway seeds are included in most beef goulash recipes, but I have never seen them in a Nigerian supermarket. Our recipe inspiration left it out as well. If you miraculously happen to have them, add a teaspoon to the dish before cooking in the slow cooker or oven.
  • This recipe is perfect for bulk cooking and can stay frozen for a month or longer.
  • For extra richness, add a little shredded cheese when serving.
